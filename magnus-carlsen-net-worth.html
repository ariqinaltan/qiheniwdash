Introduction

Magnus Carlsen, the Norwegian chess grandmaster, is currently the world’s number one chess player and has held the title of world chess champion since 2013. In this article, we will explore Carlsen’s early life and education, career, personal life, real estate, and legacy. We will also answer some of the frequently asked questions related to the world chess champion.

Early Life and Education

Magnus Carlsen was born on 30th November 1990, in Tønsberg, Norway, to Sigrun Øen and Henrik Albert Carlsen. His fascination with chess began at a very young age, and he was introduced to the game by his father at the age of five. By the age of eight, he was already playing in tournaments and beating much older players.

Carlsen received his formal education at Gjøvik University College, where he studied economics, mathematics, and business administration. However, he dropped out in 2009 to pursue a career in professional chess full-time.

Career

Carlsen’s professional chess career began when he won the Norwegian Chess Championship in 2006 at the young age of 15. He went on to win the championship title every year until 2010. In the same year, he also became the youngest player to be ranked number one by the World Chess Federation (FIDE).

In 2013, Carlsen defeated Indian chess grandmaster Viswanathan Anand to win his first world championship title. He successfully defended his title in 2014 against Anand, 2016 against Sergey Karjakin, and 2018 against Fabiano Caruana. In 2021, Carlsen once again won the world championship title by defeating Ian Nepomniachtchi.
