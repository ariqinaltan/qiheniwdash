What Happened To Aaron Hernandez Money And House?

Posted by Billy Koelling on Sunday, August 25, 2024

The tragic story of Aaron Hernandez, the former New England Patriots tight end, is one that captured the nation’s attention. His rise to fame in the NFL was meteoric, but so was his fall from grace, culminating in his arrest and conviction for the murder of Odin Lloyd in 2013. Hernandez’s subsequent suicide in prison in 2017 left many questions unanswered, particularly regarding the fate of his finances and assets, including his house. This article delves into the details of what happened to Aaron Hernandez’s money and house after his legal troubles and death.

Introduction to Aaron Hernandez’s Financial Status

Before his arrest, Aaron Hernandez was a rising star in the NFL with a promising career ahead of him. His success on the field translated into a lucrative financial situation, including a $40 million contract with the Patriots. However, his arrest and conviction had a significant impact on his financial status.

Aaron Hernandez’s NFL Contract and Earnings

  • Hernandez signed a five-year, $40 million contract extension with the Patriots in 2012.
  • He received a $12.5 million signing bonus, one of the largest ever given to an NFL tight end at the time.
  • Additional earnings came from endorsements and other football-related income.

Legal Troubles and Financial Implications

  • The Patriots released Hernandez just hours after his arrest in 2013, foregoing any future payments.
  • Endorsement deals were also quickly terminated, cutting off another source of income.
  • Legal fees for his defense would have been substantial, further depleting his assets.

The Aftermath of Conviction

Following his conviction, the financial situation for Hernandez became even more complicated. The Patriots withheld remaining payments from his contract, including his guaranteed salary and bonus money, citing a clause that allowed them to void the contract due to conduct detrimental to the best interests of professional football.

Forfeited NFL Income

  • The Patriots withheld $3.25 million of his signing bonus and his $2.46 million base salary for 2013.
  • Hernandez also lost his $82,000 workout bonus.

Legal Fees and Civil Lawsuits

  • Defense attorneys’ fees would have consumed a significant portion of his remaining assets.
  • Hernandez faced civil lawsuits from the families of the victims, seeking damages for wrongful death.

The Fate of Aaron Hernandez’s House

Aaron Hernandez’s house in North Attleborough, Massachusetts, became a central piece of his remaining estate. Purchased for $1.3 million in 2012, the 7,100-square-foot mansion with a swimming pool, theater, and ice bath, among other amenities, was put up for sale following his arrest.

Attempts to Sell the Property

  • The house was initially listed for $1.5 million in 2016 but struggled to find a buyer.
  • The price was eventually dropped to $1 million in an effort to expedite the sale.

Seizure and Sale of the House

  • In 2017, the house was still unsold at the time of Hernandez’s death.
  • The proceeds from the eventual sale were expected to be used to settle his legal debts and any judgments from civil lawsuits.

Trust for Hernandez’s Daughter

One of the most poignant aspects of the Aaron Hernandez story is the fate of his daughter, Avielle Janelle Hernandez. Concerns about her future and financial security were paramount following her father’s death.

Creation of a Trust

  • Hernandez had set up a trust for his daughter before his death, which was intended to provide for her future.
  • The trust’s details, including its value and the assets it contained, were not made public.

Impact of Legal Issues on the Trust

  • It was unclear how much of Hernandez’s remaining assets could be funneled into the trust, given the pending legal claims against his estate.
  • The trust could potentially be targeted by creditors and plaintiffs in the wrongful death lawsuits.

Settlements and Legal Claims

After Hernandez’s death, there were numerous claims against his estate. The families of Odin Lloyd and other alleged victims sought damages, and the Patriots faced pressure to pay out the remainder of Hernandez’s contract to his estate.

Claims by Victims’ Families

  • The Lloyd family filed a wrongful death lawsuit seeking to secure assets from Hernandez’s estate.
  • Settlements or court judgments in these cases could deplete the estate’s funds.

Dispute with the New England Patriots

  • The NFL Players Association filed a grievance on behalf of Hernandez’s estate against the Patriots, seeking to recover the withheld signing bonus and other monies.
  • The outcome of this grievance could significantly affect the estate’s value.

Bankruptcy and Estate Insolvency

With mounting legal claims and limited assets, the estate of Aaron Hernandez faced the possibility of insolvency. This would mean that the estate’s liabilities exceeded its assets, complicating the distribution to creditors and claimants.

Estate’s Financial Status

  • Reports suggested that Hernandez’s estate was worth very little at the time of his death.
  • Any remaining assets were likely to be consumed by legal fees and settlements.

Implications of Insolvency

  • If the estate was declared insolvent, it would follow a specific legal process to determine how remaining assets were distributed.
  • Creditors and claimants would receive payments based on the priority of their claims, with many potentially receiving only a fraction of what was owed.

FAQ Section

Did Aaron Hernandez’s daughter receive any money?

It is known that Hernandez set up a trust for his daughter, but the exact amount and whether she has received any funds from it are not publicly disclosed.

What happened to Aaron Hernandez’s NFL pension?

Hernandez’s NFL pension would typically be part of his estate, but it is unclear how much, if any, has been claimed or distributed.

Did the Patriots ever pay the remainder of Hernandez’s contract?

The Patriots withheld remaining payments after his arrest. The NFL Players Association filed a grievance on behalf of his estate to recover these funds, but the outcome is not publicly known.

Conclusion

The financial and legal aftermath of Aaron Hernandez’s life and death is complex and fraught with challenges. His once-promising NFL career and substantial earnings were quickly overshadowed by his legal troubles, leading to the forfeiture of income and assets. The fate of his house and the trust set up for his daughter are particularly poignant elements of this story, reflecting the personal toll beyond the headlines. While some details remain private or unresolved, it is clear that the financial legacy of Aaron Hernandez is one marked by loss, legal battles, and the ongoing impact on those he left behind.
