How to get perfect brows on your wedding day

Posted by Billy Koelling on Thursday, August 29, 2024

There's a lot of truth in the cliché that your brows frame and define the rest of your face, so on your wedding day it's perfectly normal to want them, and the rest of your make-up, to look their best. To help you tick one more thing off your wedding to-do list, we spoke to the experts to find out when and what you need to do to get a happily-ever-after for your brows.

5 months to go:

"Stop everything. Literally everything – no plucking, no threading, in fact no brow hair removal - and for the next few months just let it grow", say the team behind the popular HD Brow treatment. Resisting the urge to reshape or even tidy up your brows at this stage will ensure you have the maximum amount of eyebrow hairs to work with before you start styling them. It's a lot more difficult to get a shape you love otherwise.

4 months to go:

If you naturally have very thin brows and want to achieve a fuller style, you may want to consider trying an eyebrow growth serum like Revitalash's RevitaBrow, £48.95 or Benefit's BROWVO! conditioning eyebrow primer, £19. Lisa Potter-Dixon, head make-up artist and brow expert for Benefit Cosmetics, suggests applying the latter "twice a day for a fuller looking brow", as you need to use such products regularly, as far in advance of your wedding as possible, to see results.

3 months to go:

If you are thinking about getting your eyebrows microbladed, (a semi-permanent tattoo-based treatment, explained in more detail in our guide), you'll need to book in around 2-3 months before your wedding (or even sooner if you would like a trial run.) This is because the treatment is a two-step process, with a necessary 4-6 week gap between your initial and follow-up appointment. We'd recommend going to Karen Betts' clinic on Harley Street.

2 months to go:

With everything else involved in planning a wedding, don't forget to leave plenty of time to start thinking about the sort of make-up you'd like to wear, including for your eyebrows. If you haven't decided on the type of brow product you like already, it's the perfect time to try a variety - from pomades and palettes to pencils - to find out the style you like best.

1 month to go:

If you'd prefer to get your brows more naturally shaped and/or tinted, you should go for an in-depth consultation at a top brow bar about a month or so before your wedding. Look to Pinterest and Instagram for inspiration in advance, so you can take images of brows you like with you to the appointment and be informed enough to discuss the brow style you'd like.

If you choose to go-all out, with something like a HD Brow treatment, your stylist will "apply a custom-blended colour to either darken, lighten or simply tone the natural colour of your brow hair, before "mapping" your brows to design each bride's unique shape."

1-2 weeks to go:

"A well-looked after brow opens your eyes and makes you looked more groomed", reveals Lisa Potter-Dixon, so she recommends booking in to "get your brows waxed and tinted a week or so before the wedding." This will tidy up your brows and ensure they are the shape and colour you want on your big day, while leaving enough time for any redness or other issues to be soothed by the time you walk down the aisle. The same applies for having your brows threaded (and in which case we recommend the Blink Brow Bars).

On your wedding day:

Whether you are having your make-up done by an expert or doing your own, having a few brow products in your clutch - or in the bag of one of your bridesmaids - will ensure your brows are picture ready throughout the day. We'd recommend Tom Ford's Brow Definer, £69.99, to sculpt your brows, Benefit's ready, set, BROW! clear brow gel, £31.99, to keep them looking precise for longer and High Definition's Brow Highlighter, £16.50, to enhance their arch as a finishing touch.
