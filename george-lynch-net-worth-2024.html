George Lynch’s Early Life

George Lynch was born on September 28, 1954, in Spokane, Washington. He developed an interest in music at a young age and started playing the guitar when he was just 10 years old. His passion for music led him to join several bands during his teenage years, which helped him hone his skills as a guitarist.

Music Career

Lynch’s professional music career took off when he joined the rock band Dokken in the late 1970s. With Dokken, Lynch released several successful albums and gained recognition for his unique guitar playing style. After leaving Dokken, Lynch formed his own band, Lynch Mob, which also enjoyed considerable success.

Dokken Years

During his time with Dokken, Lynch contributed to several hit songs and albums. The band’s success in the 1980s significantly boosted Lynch’s popularity and wealth. Some of the band’s most successful albums include “Under Lock and Key” and “Back for the Attack,” both of which achieved platinum status.

Lynch Mob and Solo Career

After leaving Dokken, Lynch formed Lynch Mob in 1989. The band’s debut album, “Wicked Sensation,” was a commercial success. Lynch also embarked on a solo career, releasing several albums that showcased his skills as a guitarist and songwriter.

Other Sources of Income

Guitar Design and Manufacturing

Music Production and Instruction
